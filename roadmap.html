Your BCIT Program Roadmap

This roadmap shows the typical steps to go from where you are now to starting your desired BCIT program. You can use it together with your Program Match Quiz and your Goals.

This roadmap can adapt to different BCIT program areas (Tech, Health, Trades, Creative, Business).

Where you are now

Use this roadmap to track what you’ve already done and what’s left. You can adjust it with a program advisor to match your exact situation.

  • Check your current education level (e.g., Grade 11, Grade 12, post-secondary).
  • Review your Math and English background.
  • Decide on a target BCIT program (for example: CST, CIT, Nursing).

Your destination

A clear, concrete starting point: enrolled in your BCIT program with a realistic plan for graduation and your first job in the field.

  • Accepted into a full-time BCIT program.
  • Planned timeline for each term.
  • Idea of entry-level roles you can apply for after graduation.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

1

Explore & Choose Your Program

Start by researching BCIT programs that match your quiz result and interests.

  • Review your quiz results and top category (Tech, Health, Trades, Creative, Business).
  • Open a few BCIT program pages and compare admission requirements.
  • Note down 1–3 programs you are most interested in.
View My Quiz Results Explore Programs in Pathfinder Browse BCIT Programs
2

Check Admission Requirements

Every BCIT program has specific academic requirements. This step is about finding the gap between where you are now and what your target program needs.

  • Check required courses (e.g., English 12, Pre-Calculus 12, Science).
  • Check minimum grades and any required tests or assessments.
  • List which requirements you already meet and which ones you still need.

Tip: Save screenshots or notes in your own document or planner, so you can share them easily with an advisor.

3

Upgrade or Complete Missing Requirements

If you’re missing some courses or need higher grades, plan how you’ll upgrade.

  • Look at Adult Education / upgrading options (online or in-person).
  • Plan which term you’ll take each missing course.
  • Consider BCIT’s own upgrading or assessment options where available.
BCIT Entrance Requirements
4

Prepare Your Application

When you are close to meeting the requirements, start preparing your application package.

  • Create or update your MyBCIT account.
  • Gather transcripts and documents (high school, college, IELTS/TOEFL if needed).
  • Write any personal statements or supporting documents (if your program needs them).

Tip: Set reminders in your calendar for application openings and deadlines.

5

Apply & Wait for Admission Decision

After submitting, there may be a waiting period. Use this time to keep improving your skills.

  • Submit your BCIT application and pay the application fee.
  • Regularly check your email and MyBCIT for updates or requests.
  • Continue learning (online courses, practice, volunteering) while you wait.
6

Get Ready to Start at BCIT

Once accepted, organize your life so you can focus on school as much as possible.

  • Plan your finances, work schedule, and transportation.
  • Review your program’s term schedule and important dates.
  • Set personal goals for your first term (grades, networking, time management).
